Here I just want to list the traditional interleaved techs as my back up, the simulations are done in matlab following the franco maloberti’s setting, I hope I got a better understanding after the organization of papers and thesis.

In this brief, i will not put too much math, just concentrate on keywords.

Type-I block digital filtering

The block digital filtering has facilitated the design and construction of a true TI ∆Σ modulator, traditional SDM is single input and single output, the block digital filtering helps it to become a MIMO system:


some paper includes the k-factor,The k-factor is used to deal with the issue of opamp DC offsets. DC offsets are problematic in TISDM because the difference in offset between the two branches drives the modulator to instability. Reducing the cross-coupling coefficients gives more control to each parallel ∆Σ modulator, thus enabling the negative feedback loop to adjust, which maintains DC stability.

However, reducing k from unity modifies the STF and results in an increase of the quantization noise in the signal band, thereby reducing the SNDR. The choice of k is a tradeoff between the offset value that the modulator can tolerate and the achievable SNR.

We can see 2-path means we double the sampling frequency, and the SNR performance increases 15dB as we expected, because here we just use ideal components, so we didn’t put the k-factor into count.

The directly 2-path

The newbie may think repeating single path is call TI-SDM, yes, it is, but it’s not just easy. for a reminder, the 2-path will increase z to z^2, so as usually people use such kind SDM as a bandpass one. for example, you can easily find 4-path TI-SDM locating the center frequency at fs/2. what’s more, if you look at the NTF:
$$NTF=(1-z^{-2})^{2}=(1-z^{-1})^{2}(1+z^{-1})^{2}$$Therefore, a plain two-path second-oder scheme experiences a loss in SNR of 12dB. and the simulation result proves it.

And we can notice the PSD has a drop at fs/2, which also proves the bandpass feature of this kind TI-SDM.

Type-II output prediction scheme

One problem of TI-SDM is the hardware doubles as well as the mismatch problem occurs. and one special problem for TI-SDM is the quantizer domino, which means the output of one quantizer can not be put into input of another quantizer directly. this is due to the recursive operation of SDM.

However, we can use an incomplete integrator output for the input of the second quantizer in order to eliminate the quantizer domino. Incomplete integrator output is the complete integrator output without the modulator output terms.

The drawback is the large number of levels for the second quantizer(the performance usually drops) and the global feedback DAC(not understand)

Supplement

1. ZI-TI

A high-speed demux is needed at the input of the modulator to sample the input-signal and distribute it to the individual internal modulators. The demux operates at the full speed of the overall TI structure. For example, for a time-interleaving factor of 4, the demux operates at four times the speed of the individual ∆Σ modulators.

A solution to the demux problem for J=2 is to sample each branch at a different phase of the two non-overlapping clocks. Therefore, the demux is inherent in the operation of the modulator. Another more general solution that can be used for any J is called the zero-insertion interpolation technique, it keeps one input J times the original input power, and leaves other inputs as zero. for example, we use this tech on the block digital filtering TI-SDM:


And by arranging the quantizer outputs, the performance losses 2dB, becoming 79.1dB, which is still acceptable. so it has two problems:

  • The input signal amplification causes the ZITI modulator to become unstable at smaller input levels than the traditional TI modulator, because we will increase power at one input!
  • The ZITI modulator still suffers from DC offsets, so I think it’s not so suitable for low-pass case unless the DC offset tone is out of band of your interests.

2. Output prediction scheme details

By interating the output can be done within several steps, and the finall results are the same as the franco’s paper:

$$v_{1,2}(n)=\frac{0.5z^{-1}}{1-z^{-1}} [x_1(n)-y_1(n)+x_2(n)-y_2(n)]$$

$$v_{2,2}(n)=\frac{2z^{-1}}{1-z^{-1}} [2v_{1,2}(n)+0.5x_2(n)-y_1(n)-1.5y_2(n)]$$

$$y_1(n)=Q[2v_{1,2}(n)-2y_2(n)+v_{2,2}(n)]$$

$$y_1(n)=Q[v_{2,2}(n)]$$
