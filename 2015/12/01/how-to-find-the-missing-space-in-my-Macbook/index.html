how to find the missing space in my Macbook

  • Mac

Today I find 20G space disappeared in my My 128G Macbook, the problem really takes me about half hour to solve it. I didn’t store too much photos and music, and I also didn’t backup my time machine at local.

I really recommend you have a disk software in your own Mac, it can help you quickly find where the problem is:

  1. daisy disk, a beautiful software which shows your space in a pie graph, and take your money at the same money.
  2. Disk Inventory X, is a ugly disk usage utility for Mac OS X 10.3 (and later), but functional.

The whole steps:

my problem is the garbage under /private/var/vm, to open it , shift+command+G in the finder, and go to find your garbage file, and of course you have to show all the hidden files.

/var/vm/sleepimage is used to store the contents of the RAM during hibernation.
/var/vm/swapfile is the Mac Virtual Memory.

  • delete /private/var/vm/sleepimage:
  • create an empty file and name it ‘sleepimage’:
  • change its flag to immutable:
  • delete the swap files
